## Part 3 (chat 2, scope decision)

### Decision: Option 4

**Accept Qiddiya 28.1% with explicit caveat. Do not widen window. Do not drop Qiddiya. Do not switch design.**

Rationale per option:

- **Option 1 (widen to 90d).** Bad trade. Direction is already tight_null at 60d — the wider window won't change the sign, it'll admit more surface-state drift at Qiddiya, where the substrate evolves month-over-month. Buys retention by degrading atmospheric specificity. The temporal window exists exactly to prevent this.

- **Option 2 (drop Qiddiya from lead).** Loses information. Qiddiya's 16-pair tight_null at CI halfwidth 0.0055 is actually tighter than KSP's. Dropping a result for borderline retention reads as cherry-picking. Better to keep it with the retention caveat clearly labeled, and frame the 28% retention itself as a finding ("V4 is so saturated at Qiddiya we can barely find clean comparison anchors — same construction-substrate root cause as SQ2").

- **Option 3 (switch to continuous regression).** Most opinionated reject. The continuous-slope question is genuinely interesting and probably belongs in piece B somewhere, but switching to it now, mid-run, after a stop rule fires, is the move the stop rule was designed to catch. The pre-registered design is what we're testing. The continuous-slope question is a different sub-question — call it SQ3B if it's worth doing, but it's not an escape hatch for SQ3.

- **Option 4 (accept caveat, proceed).** The result is *better* than the original prediction would have given. Three readings worth holding:

  - KSP and Qiddiya tight_nulls are real nulls — halfwidths 0.0076 and 0.0055, both straddling zero, mean |Δ| ≤ 0.003. Below NDVI change-detection thresholds (~0.05+). Honest framing: *"On the operational set at these AOIs, Sen2Cor's L2A NDVI does not exhibit a measurable bias on V4-flagged scenes vs near-temporal unflagged neighbors at moderate atmospheric loadings."* Conditional, scoped.
  - This serves the umbrella question better than a confirmed bias would have. *"What can satellites honestly say"* — more than the Goyens prior would predict for NDVI-based change detection at these loadings. *"What are they missing"* — SQ4 HLS cross-check (LaSRC vs Sen2Cor), SQ8 AERONET ground truth, the Qiddiya construction-substrate finding (methodology, not atmospheric bias). Narrative arc holds.
  - Diriyah's wide_inconclusive (n=9, halfwidth 0.022) is honestly an n-problem. *"Diriyah's lower fire rate means we'd need more years or AERONET ground-truth to call this"* — that's the SQ8 hook, sitting where it should.

## Lessons reinforced

- **Stop rules are scope-decision points, not failures.** SQ3's stop rule fired at Qiddiya 28.1% retention. The agent halted, surfaced four scope options without picking, and waited for the researcher's call. That's the stop rule working **exactly as designed**, not a problem to route around. Halting in the middle of the run is the cheapest possible scope review. Pattern: when designing a sub-question, write the stop rule as a multi-option scope decision rather than a binary fail/pass — the agent's job at threshold is to pause and surface, not to choose.

- **The "different question" temptation when a stop rule fires.** When an analysis hits a stop-rule edge, the tempting move is to switch to a different design that bypasses the constraint (SQ3 paired-difference → continuous DBB-vs-NDVI regression). That switch is exactly what the stop rule is designed to catch: it conflates "we got a borderline retention" with "we should answer a different question." Pre-registered designs ship or halt on their own terms; new questions become new sub-questions. SQ3 held; the continuous-slope question, if worth doing, becomes SQ3B or an appendix.

- **Conditional nulls are findings, not setbacks.** SQ3's tight nulls at halfwidths 0.0076 (KSP) and 0.0055 (Qiddiya), both well below NDVI change-detection thresholds, are scope-bounded informative results. The honest framing names what the result *does* say (no measurable bias at this design at this loading regime on this correction chain) and what it *does not* say (Sen2Cor is universally fine; Goyens is wrong). Goyens describes a TOA / extreme-AOD regime; transfer to L2A at moderate loadings is not assumed in either direction.

- **The diagnosis is often the finding.** Qiddiya 28.1% retention isn't a sampling problem — it's a direct downstream consequence of SQ2's construction-substrate finding (V4 fires on 75% of Qiddiya scenes → small unfired pool). The same root cause is propagated through the imaging chain. Reported in §5 as a structural consequence, not a flaw. Pattern: when a stop rule fires, look upstream — the cause may already be a documented finding from a previous SQ.

- **Recon-before-build catches infra mismatches in 4 commands.** R1 (NDVI not in operational CSV), R2 (EECU estimate ≤15), R3 (column names confirmed), R4 (denominator 226/228) all resolved before any new code was written. Saved a build pass that would have referenced `scene_date` (manifest col is `acquisition_date`) and assumed NDVI columns that didn't exist. Standard for any sub-question that touches an existing pipeline.

- **Self-caught bugs validate the verification loop.** Sanity_test bug (`scene_date` vs `acquisition_date`) failed loudly with `KeyError`, never reached output. Verification discipline working. Pattern: design diagnostics that *fail loudly* on the first wrong assumption rather than silently producing plausible-looking output.

- **Pair-level bootstrap for paired difference designs.** Bootstrap on **pairs** (not scenes) is correct when neighbors can be reused across pairings — pair-level resampling handles the dependence at inference time. Standard for any future paired sub-question.

- **Two-layer findings hold up better than one-layer findings.** SQ2 (V4 detects atmospheric optical thickness — Diriyah shamal alignment confirms) plus SQ3 (that thickness does not propagate to NDVI bias at change-detection magnitude on Sen2Cor L2A — null at moderate loadings) is a more substantive piece B answer than a single bias-confirmation. The chain is internally consistent and informative. Frame as a two-layer answer to the umbrella question, not as a contradiction.

- **The smallest-n AOI is often the cleanest atmospheric prior.** Diriyah has the lowest V4 fire rate (11.8%) → the smallest paired set (n=8) → the widest CI. The AOI that's most worth measuring is the AOI we can measure least precisely from this design alone. SQ8 AERONET tightens this exactly. Pattern: when stop-rule wide-inconclusive results emerge, check whether the wide CI is an n-problem at the most informative AOI — that's a deferred-question signal, not a design failure.
