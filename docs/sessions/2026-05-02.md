## Part 3 (chat 2, scope decision)

### Decision: Option 4

**Accept Qiddiya 28.1% with explicit caveat. Do not widen window. Do not drop Qiddiya. Do not switch design.**

Rationale per option:

- **Option 1 (widen to 90d).** Bad trade. Direction is already tight_null at 60d — the wider window won't change the sign, it'll admit more surface-state drift at Qiddiya, where the substrate evolves month-over-month. Buys retention by degrading atmospheric specificity. The temporal window exists exactly to prevent this.

- **Option 2 (drop Qiddiya from lead).** Loses information. Qiddiya's 16-pair tight_null at CI halfwidth 0.0055 is actually tighter than KSP's. Dropping a result for borderline retention reads as cherry-picking. Better to keep it with the retention caveat clearly labeled, and frame the 28% retention itself as a finding ("V4 is so saturated at Qiddiya we can barely find clean comparison anchors — same construction-substrate root cause as SQ2").

- **Option 3 (switch to continuous regression).** Most opinionated reject. The continuous-slope question is genuinely interesting and probably belongs in piece B somewhere, but switching to it now, mid-run, after a stop rule fires, is the move the stop rule was designed to catch. The pre-registered design is what we're testing. The continuous-slope question is a different sub-question — call it SQ3B if it's worth doing, but it's not an escape hatch for SQ3.

- **Option 4 (accept caveat, proceed).** The result is *better* than the original prediction would have given. Three readings worth holding:

  - KSP and Qiddiya tight_nulls are real nulls — halfwidths 0.0076 and 0.0055, both straddling zero, mean |Δ| ≤ 0.003. Below NDVI change-detection thresholds (~0.05+). Honest framing: *"On the operational set at these AOIs, Sen2Cor's L2A NDVI does not exhibit a measurable bias on V4-flagged scenes vs near-temporal unflagged neighbors at moderate atmospheric loadings."* Conditional, scoped.
  - This serves the umbrella question better than a confirmed bias would have. *"What can satellites honestly say"* — more than the Goyens prior would predict for NDVI-based change detection at these loadings. *"What are they missing"* — SQ4 HLS cross-check (LaSRC vs Sen2Cor), SQ8 AERONET ground truth, the Qiddiya construction-substrate finding (methodology, not atmospheric bias).   - Diriyah's wide_inconclusive (n=9, halfwidth 0.022) is honestly an n-problem. *"Diriyah's lower fire rate means we'd need more years or AERONET ground-truth to call this"* — that's the SQ8 hook, sitting where it should.

## Lessons reinforced

- **Stop-rule discipline holds across four sub-questions in different shapes.** SQ3 retention, SQ4B Arm B coverage, SQ5 retention, SQ8 AERONET R1 — each fired differently, each produced a sub-question deferral rather than a mid-run design switch. Pattern is robust across sub-question types: paired-design retention, cross-sensor coverage, seasonal stratification, ground-truth availability. Halting in the middle of the run is the cheapest possible scope review.

- **Halt-with-receipt as a sub-question shape.** SQ4B Arm B → SQ4C deferral preserved the L30 coverage probe in git history (commit `71327ec`). SQ5 halt preserved UVAI quartile labels, retention probe, contingency table, and seasonal stratification figure as committed receipts. The receipts are themselves piece B substance — SQ5's seasonal-stratification methodology finding and Q4∧¬V4 anchor cell would not exist without the halt being shipped as a finding rather than discarded. Pattern: when a sub-question halts on a structural constraint, the recon artifacts ride into the ship as findings.

- **Dual-criterion pre-registration as a methodology pattern.** SQ8 surfaced the disagreement between significance criterion (CI excludes zero) and magnitude criterion (|β × IQR| exceeds operational threshold) at high-n low-R² regressions. Pre-register both; expect potential disagreement; surface explicitly when it fires; decide which is load-bearing for the umbrella question (operational-magnitude is load-bearing for change-monitoring use cases). Document the disagreement when it occurs as a methodology observation rather than resolving by override.

- **Power-confirmation reading of significant-but-tiny regression effects.** When a regression detects a statistically significant effect that is 25× below the operational-significance threshold, the Bayesian-inverse reading is that the test had power to find a small effect if one existed and that is what it found. This strengthens, rather than undermines, an operational null established by other designs. SQ8's −0.0018 NDVI per IQR AOD reads as power-confirmation of SQ3/SQ4/SQ4B's operational null, not as Goyens transfer.

- **Multi-layer findings hold up better than one-layer findings.** SQ2 + SQ3 + SQ4 + SQ4B + SQ5 (halt-with-receipt) + SQ8 is a five-design answer chain to the umbrella question. Each layer rules out a candidate explanation and tightens the scope claim. Frame as a multi-layer chain in piece B prose, not as a sequence of independent findings. The chain transitions from scope-conditional null at moderate loadings (SQ3) to scope-conditional null across both correction chains (SQ4) to scope-conditional null across both correction chains AND both NIR bands (SQ4B) to demonstration that paired designs cannot probe high-AOD at this site (SQ5) to power-confirmed operational null at high reanalysis-AOD (SQ8). The piece B headline is the unconditional result of the five-design convergence.

- **Recon-before-build saves build passes consistently.** SQ4 R1 (HLS coverage), SQ4B R1 (L30 coverage probe → halt), SQ5 R1/R2 (UVAI distribution + retention probe → halt), SQ8 R1 (AERONET station survey → halt) all detected structural constraints before any new code was written. The halt outcomes are all design-level findings, not infra failures. Standard for any sub-question that touches an existing pipeline.

- **In-build corrections vs stop-rule halts are different categories.** SQ4's `coll.first()` → `coll.mosaic()` switch and SQ8's CAMS ±60min → ±120min window expansion are in-build corrections (caught by sanity checks during execution, fixed and re-run, no scope decision). SQ3/SQ4B/SQ5/SQ8 halts are scope-decision points (stop, surface, await researcher call). Both are valuable; the difference is whether the issue can be fixed in-design or requires re-scoping.

- **The diagnosis is often the finding.** Qiddiya 28.1% retention at SQ3 was a direct downstream consequence of SQ2's construction-substrate finding. SQ4B Arm B's coverage failure was a structural cadence mismatch. SQ5's retention failure was Riyadh's UVAI seasonality. SQ8's AERONET halt was the absence of operational stations within 500km. In each case, the diagnosis carries forward as a finding (or as methodology context for the next sub-question).

Pattern: scripts are source of truth for infrastructure; this doc's "What works right now" section needs verification when describing data sources.
