## Lessons reinforced

- **Researcher confirmation pass is load-bearing, not procedural.** 27.9% disagreement rate translated into AUC drops across all four variants. Preliminary V3/V4 numbers (0.93/0.92) overstated by ~9 points each. Without the confirmation pass, piece B would have shipped on AI-pattern-cleaner-than-reality numbers and reviewers would have surfaced exactly this concern. Doing the pass before submission is the move that turns this from "self-published dashboard" into "knows how to do science."

- **AI pre-labeling produces optimistic class separation.** Pattern-matchers push borderline scenes toward the nearest cluster centroid; real visual judgment is messier and that mess is signal. Confirmed across all four SQ1B variants 2026-05-01. This is a finding about AI calibration in general, worth naming in piece B's methodology section.

- **AOI-dependent AI bias direction is a real finding.** Construction-active AOIs (Qiddiya, KSP) → AI over-flags haze (substrate confusion). Desert-edge AOI (Diriyah) → AI under-flags real dust. This pattern wouldn't surface from a smaller calibration set; the 43-scene SQ1C pass was load-bearing for finding it. Nuanced and substantive — not "AI is bad," but "AI bias is conditional on surface context."

- **Cold-protocol audits can return null results.** 4/6 cold-confirmed labels agreed with AI; 2/6 disagreed in directions consistent with the AOI-level pattern. Bias_exposure during pre-labeling did not produce a detectably different judgment pattern at this n. The methodology footnote disclosure stays (the procedural drift was real), but the contamination hypothesis itself returned a negative result — also a finding, just smaller.

- **V1 collapse is structural, not noise.** With n_pos=2, the formula trivially separated 2 outliers from 49 cleans (mechanical AUC). With n_pos=5 spanning a realistic UVAI/DBB range, it can't. The Lolli DBB index is not a univariate heavy_dust detector at Riyadh-region atmospheric loadings — that's a piece B finding, not a setback.

- **Qiddiya 2022-04-10 is publishable on its own.** Confirmed clean despite UVAI +2.07, surviving cold-protocol blind labeling. Single-scene direct demonstration of visual-atmospheric / UVAI decoupling over bright desert surfaces. Goyens 2024 underestimation pattern, validated through a contamination-resistant labeling protocol. Strong candidate for piece B introduction.

- **The "didn't do anything yesterday" risk is real and the recovery move is small steps not big ones.** 5 thumbnails to test the rubric, then the rest of the pass. Avoidance shows up as questions about the rubric, not as direct refusal — recognize the pattern.

- **Long prompts to long work.** SQ2 prompt was ~250 lines: worktree guardrail + recon + build (3 scripts) + run + verify + 3 atomic commits + final report, single review point at end. Ran clean end-to-end. Confirmed working pattern at SQ1C tooling, SQ1C confirmation rerun, and now SQ2 operational. Interleaved approval gates burn momentum; recon-build-verify in one shot does not.

- **Cross-check patterns surface drift mechanisms.** The 1e-4 tolerance vs `sq1bc_combined` was the only reason the GEE baseline-reprocessing drift surfaced. Without it, pixel content would have shifted silently across runs and only mattered when classifications eventually flipped. Pattern: any time a scene has been computed before, cross-check the new compute against the old at tight tolerance, surface failures honestly. This caught GEE drift mechanism #3 — now in operational guardrails.

- **The plots tell stories the summary stats undersell.** Qiddiya's +0.10 baseline is the methodology finding made visible — looking at the time-series plot, the right question is "what makes a Qiddiya scene drop *below* threshold," not "what makes one fire." That's a different framing than the 75% rate alone communicates. Per-AOI plots are non-optional for piece B.

- **Internal cross-validation between methods that didn't share inputs is rare and load-bearing.** SQ1C cold-protocol blind labels (no UVAI, no AI) and SQ2 continuous DBB (no labels at decision time) independently identified the same Diriyah peak event (2022-05-10). Two methods, no shared decision-time inputs, agreement on the peak. Name this kind of agreement explicitly when it surfaces.
