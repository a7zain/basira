# 2026-04-28 — SQ1D Lolli audit, AOI fix, reference selection

## Headline
Audited SQ1's `dbb_index.py` against Lolli 2024 paper. Found it's not
Lolli's DBB — it's a single-image visible-vs-SWIR ratio on L2A inputs,
while Lolli's actual formula is a per-band TOA differential normalized
by L2A reference reflectance. Pivoted piece B to faithful Lolli port via
GEE (CDSE 503'd for second day). During reference scene selection,
discovered KSP bbox was 2x oversized; tightened to actual King Salman
Park boundaries.

## What happened

CDSE Sentinel Hub returned 503 again. Used the downtime productively:
retrieved Lolli 2024 PDF, audited SQ1's DBB implementation against the
paper. Found three faithfulness gaps: (1) SQ1 uses single image, Lolli
uses two (test + reference); (2) SQ1 uses L2A surface reflectance, Lolli
uses L1C TOA; (3) SQ1 normalizes by single-scene visible+SWIR sum, Lolli
normalizes by per-band L2A reference reflectance. Pivoted to faithful
port via Google Earth Engine.

Web research confirmed the contribution slot is real: Goyens 2024 evaluated
Sen2Cor AOT against AERONET globally but didn't separately validate the
Arabian Peninsula bright-desert regime. No published Sentinel-2 dust
index has been calibrated for AP. Lolli's three test sites (Potenza,
Barcelona, Tenerife) are dark surfaces; he didn't test transferability
to bright desert. Piece B framing strengthened: faithful Lolli port +
AP recalibration + sensitivity to surface-temporal drift.

SQ1D Part A: identified top-5 reference candidates per AOI from visually-
labeled "clean" scenes. Part A.5: cross-checked candidates against
TROPOMI UVAI. Qiddiya and Diriyah locked cleanly. KSP showed every
visually-clean date had UVAI mean ≥ 1.25 — substantial absorbing aerosol.
Hypothesis: construction substrate visually mimics haze, labels are
contaminated.

Part A.6: widened KSP search to all 76 months by TROPOMI UVAI — found
65 sub-0.3 candidates the labels had missed. Part A.7: same widen-search
for Qiddiya. Part A.6.1: discovered atmospheric cleanness regime change —
50/65 clean columns at KSP fall in 2020-2021, only 1 from 2022 onward.
Same pattern at Qiddiya (38/38 negative-UVAI candidates pre-2022).
This is a finding worth a paragraph in piece B's discussion section.

Reference scene selection trade-off identified: cleanest atmosphere
(2021-01-10 at both AOIs) vs surface-temporal proximity to test scenes
(construction-active AOIs have substantial surface change between 2021
and 2026). Rendered RGB thumbnails for all candidate dates per AOI.
Visual review confirmed dramatic surface change between 2020 and 2024
at Qiddiya. Picked recent acceptable references.

Visual review of KSP candidates revealed broader problem: I initially
read the KSP bbox as encompassing King Khalid Airport + urban Riyadh +
unrelated megaproject. After Claude Code's geographic research,
correction: bbox actually centered on the former Riyadh Air Base
(decommissioned 1980s, demolished 2020-22), which IS the King Salman
Park site. What I saw as "airport" was old runway features in pre-2022
imagery; what I saw as "unrelated construction" was KSP's own development.

But: bbox was still 2x oversized (29.93 km² vs published 16.6 km²) and
encompassed surrounding urban perimeter. Tightened to a clean 4.07x4.07km
rectangle centered on Wikipedia KSP coordinates. Verified the new bbox
renders the actual KSP construction footprint via fresh Sentinel-2
thumbnail. Geometry change committed.

## Lessons reinforced
- AOI definitions from public landmark names need visual verification,
  not just centroid checks. Render thumbnails before locking.
- Visual labels can be confounded by construction substrate at
  construction-active AOIs. Cross-check with TROPOMI UVAI before treating
  labels as ground truth.
- Reference scene cleanness is necessary but not sufficient for piece B —
  surface-temporal representativeness matters too at construction AOIs.
- Visually misidentifying old-runway-features as active-airport caused
  me to recommend dropping KSP. Ahmed pushed back, Claude Code's
  geographic research corrected the misread, KSP retained with tightened
  bbox. The pushback was right.
- CDSE 503 outage being multi-day pushed us to GEE pivot. Net positive —
  GEE is structurally better fit for piece B regardless of CDSE state.
