## Lessons reinforced

From Part 1:

- **GEE catalog backfill is a real silent-drift mechanism.** Yesterday's helper-port self-test surfaced it; today's manifest lock defends against it. 23/24 byte-identical thumbnails after a port that should have been pure code-motion was the tell — pixel content of one scene moved without the script changing. If the helper port had gone cleanly, this hole would have lived another four months and surfaced at piece B's reproducibility-review moment.
- **Mosaic-vs-system_index is a tile-edge subtlety.** First iteration of manifest-authoritative renderers used `system:index` single-image clips and produced 0.33% pixel drift on Qiddiya thumbnails (multi-tile bbox), zero drift on KSP (single-tile bbox). Standard pattern locked: date-based fetch + `assert_manifest_match(system_index)` precondition. The index is for verification, the date is for the fetch.
- **Skip-with-marker guard earned its keep within 24 hours.** Hardened 2026-04-29 (commit `ec88f93`); deployed to test renderers 2026-04-30 morning; fired correctly on 2 KSP no-data scenes 2026-04-30 evening. Patterns that defend against a known failure mode pay back fast when the mode recurs.

From Part 2:

- **Web-search the outage when the platform won't talk to you.** Four days of CDSE 503 looked like a service degradation pattern; one search surfaced the announced "Major upgrade of CDSE networking on 28/04/2026" planned for the exact day the outages started. Post-upgrade residual instability, platform-wide, not user-specific. Cheap diagnostic skipped on days 1-3 because the mitigation pattern (abort and continue) didn't require root cause; cost was the four-day-blocker framing that was wrong.
- **Scripts are the source of truth for infrastructure descriptions.** CLAUDE.md said "TROPOMI UVAI via Sentinel Hub on CDSE" while all three AOI scripts had been on GEE for weeks. The drift surfaced only because the CDSE 503 forced an alternate-path investigation — without the outage, the misalignment could have lived another month and shown up at piece B writeup time. Verify infrastructure descriptions against actual scripts, especially when reaching for them at session start.
- **STOP-GATEs make alternate-path proposals safer.** Unit 1 of Part 2 was set up to abort the rest of the prompt if Spearman ρ < 0.85 between SH-described and GEE-actual UVAI. The result (ρ = 1.0000) was the strongest possible pass — and revealed the documentation drift along the way. The gate was never going to fail on its real purpose, but it surfaced a finding that would have been missed if the prompt had just charged ahead.
- **The §7 SZA-aware month-bin constraint is doing real work.** KSP's allowed-months filter narrowed to only 01/02/07/12, dropping the candidate pool from full UVAI to 70. That tightness is a feature (no §7 confound contamination in the calibration set) but is also a piece-B-limitations note (V3 threshold calibrates a narrower phenomenon than annual dust).

From Part 3:

- **V2 failure isn't a setback when it's the third validation of a known finding.** The Qiddiya bidirectional construction-substrate contamination has now been seen via (a) SQ1D Pass 5 visually-blind relabel disagreement pattern, (b) Part B' sensitivity check showing primary-vs-alternate ρ=0.92 with consistent label-vs-formula structure, and (c) V2 pooled AUC drop to 0.711 even at n_pos=23. Three independent lines, same effect. This belongs in piece B's discussion as a finding, not framed as a result we couldn't get.

Disclose in prose as "no bootstrap variance under current resampling," not "infinite confidence." V4 at CI=0.018 is the sturdier statistic to lead with.
